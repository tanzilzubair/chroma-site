Chroma 1.0

Hello and welcome to Chroma!

Now, before we get to the good stuff, I'd like to take a moment to thank you for choosing to use my little app, and tell you that this choice means a lot to me, and that I won't let you down. With that being said, let's move on to the changes!

Do note however, that, for iOS users, this will be the first version of the app publicly available, so this isn't going to be a list of changes so much so as a list of features.

A Completely Overhauled UI

Taking the best of what made the previous UI good, and infusing it with custom, purpose-built designs honed with industry standard best practices and principles: built to be an absolute joy to use.

Listen, I could talk about it all day, but I think we'd both much rather that you go and experience it for yourself!

  • A more unified, minimal UI with complementary colors, beautiful fonts and everything from buttons, labels, menus, and animations; everything, redesigned and rebuilt better than ever.
  • Sounds and haptics; the app has both now. Carefully made, tested and implemented from the ground up to enrich the user experience: giving more context to available actions and more intuitive feedback on chosen ones.
  • Autocomplete that is straight up beautifully animated, full of personality, and that Just Worksâ„¢. That's all I'll say, because it's easier if you go and experience it for yourself!
  • So many small and medium sized things, we'd be here all day if I listed them; so, go ahead and just enjoy them

A Radically Redesigned Backend

Because although taking on this much work at once may have caused me to question my sanity, err several times, what good is overhauling one part of the app, if its forced to drag around the obsolete and burdensome other half everywhere it goes?

  • The Apollo Engine, a redesigned and completely rewritten, modular, query and response parsing engine, with more concise, faster running logic than ever before, more unit-conversion options on top of the current ones, and of course, more resiliency to errors.
  • Distributed, load-balanced and beefed up servers, running on demand, all over the world, enabling ultra-fast, sub-second responses globally.
  • A lot of minor improvements, logic streamlining, bug fixes, performance improvements, etc. etc. to round out the backend redesign.
  • Many, many smaller and larger changes under the hood, that enable some of the new features coming soon!

Other Improvements

  • A whole lot of personality; specifically, in the little things: a trivia section curated, formatted and fact-checked by yours truly, and, all too familiar locations as examples on the autocomplete field, for example.
  • Advanced configuration settings, for all you weather buffs who want them.
  • A brand new splash screen, app icon, app store descriptions and images, among other things, to really complete the redesign.
